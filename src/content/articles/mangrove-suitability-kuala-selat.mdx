Mangrove rehabilitation without spatial intelligence is planting in the dark.
Not every degraded fringe is ready for replanting — some areas are permanently
backlogged by tidal disruption, others lack the bare-earth elevation to sustain
seedling establishment. This analysis asks a precise question: **of the 349 hectares
in the PRM rehabilitation concession, where can mangroves actually grow?**

The answer required three data layers: Sentinel-1 SAR (to detect inundation dynamics),
Sentinel-2 optical imagery (to validate vegetation response), and FABDEM
(to characterise bare-earth elevation within the intertidal window).

The most diagnostic signature is **backlogged tidal** zones. In the dry season,
their VH backscatter sits in the mid-range (−16 dB), indistinguishable from
sparse mangrove. In the wet season, they jump strongly toward the specular
reflection end (−10.5 dB) — the double-bounce mechanism from standing water
beneath residual woody structure. This wet-season surge, combined with a
persistently high absolute VH value (> −15 dB), forms the basis of the
Class 4 threshold.

> **Field validation note:** Class 4 polygons identified by SAR double-bounce
> were ground-truthed in February 2025. In all cases, field teams confirmed
> the presence of standing brackish water 10–30 cm deep during the survey,
> with no viable propagule establishment observed. The double-bounce criterion
> was retained as the primary Class 4 indicator.

## Classification decision tree

The full four-class decision rule applied in GEE is summarised below:

| Class | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **Class 1 — Highly suitable** | NDVI > 0.45 OR (VH −18 to −12 dB AND ΔVH < 2 dB AND FABDEM 0.1–0.8 m) |
| **Class 2 — Suitable** | NDVI 0.25–0.45 AND FABDEM 0.0–1.2 m AND no double-bounce |
| **Class 3 — Marginally suitable** | FABDEM 0.0–1.2 m AND (NDVI < 0.25 OR ΔVH 2–3 dB) |
| **Class 4 — Not suitable** | ΔVH > +3 dB AND VH > −15 dB (double-bounce) OR FABDEM < 0 m OR FABDEM > 1.5 m |

## Export specification

The final output was exported from GEE as a multi-band GeoTIFF at 10 m
resolution in EPSG:4326, with the following band stack:

```
Band 1: Suitability class (1–4, integer)
Band 2: VH dry-season median (float, dB)
Band 3: VH wet-season median (float, dB)
Band 4: ΔVH (float, dB)
Band 5: NDVI median (float)
Band 6: NDWI median (float)
Band 7: FABDEM elevation (float, metres)
```

This seven-band stack allows downstream users to re-threshold any individual
criterion without re-running the full GEE workflow, and supports integration
with the InundationMap V2 FastAPI tile server for live web visualisation.

## Methods summary

**Sentinel-1:** GRD IW, VV+VH, ascending orbit. Dry composite: August–October
2023. Wet composite: January–March 2024. Processed with the GEE Sentinel-1
Toolbox (border noise removal, refined Lee speckle filter, terrain correction
via SRTM 30 m).

**Sentinel-2:** Level-2A SR, cloud-masked (s2cloudless), median composite over
the same periods as Sentinel-1. Bands: B2 (Blue), B3 (Green), B4 (Red),
B8 (NIR), B11 (SWIR1).

**FABDEM:** v1.2, 30 m resolution, referenced to EGM2008 geoid. Resampled
to 10 m using bilinear interpolation to match Sentinel pixel grid.

**Polygon asset:** `PRM_2026_Riau_349Ha` — 31 hand-delineated polygons
uploaded as a GEE FeatureCollection. All raster statistics computed per polygon
using `ee.Reducer.median()` with a 10 m pixel scale.
