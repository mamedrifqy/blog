Riau Province sits on one of the largest tropical peatland complexes on Earth. 
When these peat soils dry out, they become fuel — not just at the surface, 
but metres deep, sustaining smouldering fires that can burn for weeks.

The key question this analysis asks: **how tightly coupled is fire to ENSO, and 
where does that coupling break down?**

## ENSO–Hotspot correlation, 2015–2025

The dual-axis chart below overlays the Oceanic Niño Index (ONI) — the standard 
ENSO metric — with monthly hotspot counts across all Riau regencies. El Niño 
years (shaded orange) correlate strongly with fire peaks; La Niña years (blue) 
suppress fire activity.

<EnsoChart 
  data={ENSO_DATA}
  title="ONI Index vs Monthly Hotspot Count · Riau Province 2015–2025"
  client:visible 
/>

The Pearson correlation coefficient across this dataset is **r = 0.72** — 
statistically significant (p < 0.01), but not deterministic. Three important 
caveats emerge from the data:

**1. The 2019 anomaly.** Despite a weak ENSO signal (ONI ≈ +0.3), 2019 recorded 
nearly as many hotspots as the 2015 super El Niño. The preceding two years had 
depleted peat water tables, priming the landscape for fire even without an 
extreme climate forcing.

**2. Spatial heterogeneity.** Indragiri Hilir responds almost immediately to ONI 
anomalies; Rokan Hulu — further inland with less degraded peat — shows a 
dampened and lagged response.

**3. Land-use matters as much as climate.** The highest hotspot densities 
consistently cluster in oil palm concession boundaries, suggesting that 
land-clearing practices amplify climate-driven fire risk.

## Methods

Hotspot data was sourced from the FIRMS archive (MODIS MCD14ML + VIIRS VNP14IMGTDL) 
clipped to Riau Province boundaries. Monthly aggregations were computed by 
regency polygon using a spatial join in GeoPandas. ONI values are from NOAA's 
CPC 3-month running mean dataset.

Correlation analysis used both Pearson and Spearman coefficients. All analysis 
code is available in this project's `/notebooks` directory.

> **Data note:** Hotspot counts in this article use the 2025 FIRMS archive 
> release. Earlier publications used different cloud-masking parameters — 
> direct comparisons with pre-2023 literature should account for this.
